# Poulet Rôti 

This is one of the most classic French family meals. The beauty is its simplicity: good chicken, butter, garlic, herbs, and potatoes roasting underneath.

_Serves 4–6_

🛒 *Shopping List*
1 whole chicken (about 1.5–1.8 kg)
1 kg potatoes (Maris Piper, King Edward, or similar)
1 lemon
1 head of garlic
50 g butter, softened
Fresh thyme (or dried thyme)
Fresh parsley (optional)
Olive oil
Salt
Black pepper

👨‍🍳 *Preparation*

1. Heat the oven
Preheat to 200°C (180°C fan).
2. Prepare the chicken
Pat the chicken dry with kitchen paper.
Rub it all over with softened butter.
Season generously with salt and pepper.
Put inside the cavity:
Half a lemon
2–3 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
A few sprigs of thyme
3. Prepare the potatoes
Cut potatoes into large chunks.
Place them in a roasting tray.
Toss with:
2 tbsp olive oil
Salt
Pepper
A few thyme sprigs
4. Roast
Place the chicken on top of the potatoes (or on a rack above them).
Roast for about 1 hour 20 minutes to 1 hour 40 minutes, depending on size.
Every 20–30 minutes, spoon some juices over the chicken and potatoes.
5. Check it's done
Pierce the thickest part of the thigh.
The juices should run clear, not pink.
Internal temperature should reach about 75°C.
6. Rest
Remove from oven.
Cover loosely with foil.
Let rest for 15 minutes before carving.

🍽️ *Serve with*

A typical French family might add:
A green salad with vinaigrette
Crusty baguette
Cheese afterwards, such as Camembert or Comté

## *French Grandmother's Trick*

Scatter the unpeeled cloves from the whole head of garlic around the potatoes before roasting. By the end, they'll be soft and sweet. Squeeze them onto bread or mix them into the potatoes.
The result is exactly the sort of comforting Saturday dinner you might find in many French homes: crispy skin, juicy chicken, roasted potatoes soaked in chicken juices, a simple salad, and plenty of bread. Bon appétit! 🇫🇷🍗🥔🍷
